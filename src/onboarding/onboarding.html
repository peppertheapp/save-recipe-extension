What Pepper can (and can't) do

Chrome warned you it can "read and change data on all websites." Here's the plain-English version.

Pepper adds its Save button to recipe pages and reads the page to find the recipe — that's all. The page content is checked right in your browser to spot a recipe; it's never stored anywhere and is discarded the moment you leave the page. Nothing is sent to Pepper until you click Save, and then only the recipe itself. It never touches your logins, your accounts, or anything you type.
